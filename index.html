About

Duling Xu is currently a Ph.D. student at the School of Information, Renmin University of China. Her supervisors are Prof. Tong Li and Prof. Wei Lu.

Her research interests include computer networks and database management systems. She currently focuses on optimizing application performance in cross-domain scenarios from a network perspective, including adaptive routing strategies, latency-aware task offloading, and intelligent traffic engineering for distributed environments.

Research Experiences

High-Performance Geo-Distributed Collective Communication Framework

2024.7 - Present
  • Construct the GeoCoCo framework to enhance performance and expand distributed application communication scenarios.
  • Study dynamic path selection algorithms to reduce communication latency and improve reliability in complex network environments.
  • Explore data aggregation and filtering techniques to reduce bandwidth consumption and improve geo-distributed communication efficiency.

Performance-Oriented Leader Management for Geo-Distributed Consensus Protocol

2023.11 - 2024.7
  • Addressed leader management challenges in geo-distributed databases.
  • Proposed GeoLM, which considers network and application dynamics to achieve seamless leader handover.
  • Evaluated GeoLM against baselines and state-of-the-art protocols.

Survey on Transmission Technology in Wide Area Deterministic Networking

2023.10 - 2024.1
  • Reviewed the connotation of deterministic networks and related traditional technologies.
  • Analyzed new applications, features, challenges, and goals of wide area deterministic networks.
